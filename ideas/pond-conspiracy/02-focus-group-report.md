## üí° Recommendations

### HIGH PRIORITY (Critical for Success)

**1. Reduce MVP Scope by 30-40%**
- Cut: Environmental hazards (water level changes, weather effects)
- Cut: Secret boss (prove basic bosses work first)
- Cut: Multiple endings (ship with ONE complete ending, add more post-launch)
- Cut: Visual mutation effects (polish, not essential)
- Cut: Dynamic music (start with good static soundtrack)
- **Keep**: Core combat, conspiracy board, 15 mutations, 2-3 bosses, one ending

**2. Prototype Conspiracy Board in Week 1**
- This is your unique selling point AND biggest risk
- Build paper prototype or clickable mockup
- Test with 5-10 target users BEFORE implementing in-game
- Iterate based on feedback: Is it intuitive? Satisfying? Too text-heavy?

**3. Create Free Web Demo (5-Minute Capped)**
- Prove combat feel immediately
- Let players experience first mutation choice
- End with "Unlock full conspiracy in the complete game"
- Use for marketing: easy to share, low friction

**4. Establish Real Environmental Partnership EARLY**
- Contact orgs BEFORE launch (Waterkeeper Alliance, Sierra Club, etc.)
- Get scientific review of data logs
- Set up transparent donation tracking
- Document everything for marketing credibility

**5. Plan for Early Access, Not 1.0 Launch**
- 6-8 weeks = vertical slice, not complete game
- Launch Early Access at $10, gather feedback, iterate
- Roadmap to 1.0: add endings, mutations, boss, polish
- Increase price to $15 at 1.0 launch
- This reduces risk and builds community

### MEDIUM PRIORITY (Important, Not Urgent)

**6. Design for Streamability from Day 1**
- Built-in highlight/clip markers for exciting moments
- Quick-summary option for conspiracy board (for streams)
- Controller support (Steam Deck is huge in this demographic)
- Performance target: 60fps on mid-tier hardware

**7. Community Building Pre-Launch**
- Discord server for devlogs and feedback
- Weekly Twitter/Reddit updates showing progress
- Invite playtesters from roguelike communities
- Partner with eco-conscious gaming YouTubers for coverage

**8. Accessibility is Not Optional**
- Colorblind modes (protanopia, deuteranopia, tritanopia)
- Rebindable controls
- Difficulty assist toggles (adaptive difficulty is good, but also explicit options)
- Text size options
- Screen shake toggle

### LOW PRIORITY (Post-Launch Content)

**9. Roadmap for Post-Launch**
- Month 1-3: Bug fixes, balance, quality of life
- Month 4-6: Second ending path, new mutations, daily challenges
- Month 7-9: Third ending, boss rush mode, achievements
- Month 10-12: 1.0 launch, final content, polish

**10. Long-Term Growth**
- Steam Workshop modding support (Year 2)
- Co-op mode exploration (if community demands it)
- Console ports (Switch is natural fit)
- Potential DLC: "Saltwater Conspiracy" expansion

## ‚ö†Ô∏è Risk Mitigation

### Risk: Conspiracy Board UX Fails
**Mitigation**:
- Prototype and test in Week 1
- Paper prototype first (cheap iteration)
- 10 user tests before implementation
- Design for skimmability (TL;DR summaries for streamers)
- Make it beautiful (screenshot-worthy)

### Risk: Combat Doesn't Feel Good
**Mitigation**:
- Reference Vampire Survivors frame-perfect
- Hire experienced game feel designer (contract if needed)
- Playtest combat loop before adding any other features
- Use Vampire Survivors GDC talks as blueprint
- Screen shake, particles, audio feedback = non-negotiable

### Risk: Tone Shift Doesn't Land
**Mitigation**:
- Hire narrative designer with comedy ‚Üí dark experience (spec: Undertale, Stanley Parable)
- Test emotional arc with focus groups
- Don't explain the joke (trust players)
- Show systems-driven message, not explicit lectures
- Multiple tonal passes in editing

### Risk: Environmental Message Feels Preachy
**Mitigation**:
- Partner with scientists for fact-checking
- "Show don't tell" design principle
- Let players discover complicity organically
- Data logs: atmospheric writing, not manifestos
- True Ending shows systemic change, not individual hero narrative

### Risk: Over-Scoping Kills Timeline
**Mitigation**:
- Use provided scope reduction recommendations
- Weekly sprint reviews with hard feature cut decisions
- "If in doubt, cut it" mentality
- Post-launch roadmap captures cut features
- Early Access model allows iteration

### Risk: Launch Buzz Fails
**Mitigation**:
- Free web demo (viral sharing potential)
- Partner with streamers for pre-launch coverage
- Eco-conscious gaming media outreach (unique angle)
- Reddit r/roguelikes, r/IndieGaming presence
- Steam Next Fest participation (if timeline allows)
